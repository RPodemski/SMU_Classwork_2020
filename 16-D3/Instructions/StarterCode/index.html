Correlations Discovered Between the Following Variables

Poverty v. Healthcare

These two variables appear to have a positive correlation. As the Poverty percentage increases, the Lack of Healthcare also increases. Texas is the main outlier though, with the hightest lack of healthcare out of all the states.


Age v. Smoking

There does not appear to be a correlation between Age and Smoking, although all but one state falls between the age of 33 and 44. Utah is the outlier with an age of 30.5 and the lowest smoking percentage.


Income v. Obesity

Between these two variables there appears to be a negative correlation. As Income increases, Obesity decreases. Alaska, Maryland, and Colorado appear to be outliers.
